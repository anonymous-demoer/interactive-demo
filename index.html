Melodia

Training-Free Music Editing Guided by Attention Probing in Diffusion Models

Paper Code Audio Examples

Abstract

Existing music editing methods often struggle to alter attributes like timbre, genre, or mood without compromising the original music's temporal structure. To address this, we introduce Melodia, a novel training-free framework that enables precise music editing by manipulating a pre-trained text-to-music diffusion model. Our approach stems from an in-depth probing analysis of the attention layers within AudioLDM 2, which reveals that self-attention maps are crucial for preserving structural integrity (e.g., melody and rhythm), while cross-attention maps encode musical characteristics. Melodia leverages this insight by selectively replacing self-attention key and query matrices from the source audio during the denoising process of the target audio. This technique effectively guides the generation to maintain the original structure while adhering to the new textual prompt, without requiring any descriptive text of the source music. Comprehensive objective and subjective evaluations demonstrate that Melodia significantly outperforms state-of-the-art baselines in balancing textual adherence and structural preservation.

Attention Mechanism Ablation Study

Ablation on Timbre Transfer: Drum to Violin

Direct Generation

Generated from the target prompt "a solo violin" only, without any source audio influence. This serves as the target timbre/style reference.

DDIM Inversion (No Replacement)

The source audio is inverted and then denoised with the target prompt, but without applying our attention replacement. Structure is poorly preserved.

SA Replacement (Layers 8-14)

Only Self-Attention from layers 8-14 is replaced. This preserves the core temporal structure while allowing timbre to change.

SA Replacement (All Layers)

Self-Attention from all layers is replaced. Structure is strongly preserved, but some source timbre may leak into the result.

CA Replacement (Layers 8-14)

Only Cross-Attention from layers 8-14 is replaced. The result largely ignores the source structure and resembles direct generation.

CA Replacement (All Layers)

Cross-Attention from all layers is replaced. The output completely disregards the source structure, confirming CA's role in encoding textual concepts.

Ablation on Timbre Transfer: Clarinet to Trombone

Source Audio

Original Clarinet

The source audio used for the editing task.

Direct Generation

Generated from the target prompt "a solo trombone" only. This is the target timbre reference.

DDIM Inversion (No Replacement)

Standard inversion and denoising without attention manipulation. Fails to preserve the source's melodic structure.

SA Replacement (Melodia)

Our proposed method. Replaces self-attention to preserve structure while changing timbre. This is the optimal result.
