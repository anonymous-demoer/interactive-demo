Melodia

Training-Free Music Editing Guided by Attention Probing in Diffusion Models

Abstract

Existing music editing methods often struggle to alter attributes like timbre, genre, or mood without compromising the original music's temporal structure. To address this, we introduce Melodia, a novel training-free framework that enables precise music editing by manipulating a pre-trained text-to-music diffusion model. Our approach stems from an in-depth probing analysis of the attention layers within AudioLDM 2, which reveals that self-attention maps are crucial for preserving structural integrity (e.g., melody and rhythm), while cross-attention maps encode musical characteristics. Melodia leverages this insight by selectively replacing self-attention key and query matrices from the source audio during the denoising process of the target audio. This technique effectively guides the generation to maintain the original structure while adhering to the new textual prompt, without requiring any descriptive text of the source music. Comprehensive objective and subjective evaluations demonstrate that Melodia significantly outperforms state-of-the-art baselines in balancing textual adherence and structural preservation.

Attention Mechanism Ablation Study

Ablation studies demonstrating the distinct roles of Self-Attention (SA) and Cross-Attention (CA) and justifying the core mechanism of Melodia.

Ablation 1: Drum to Violin(from Figure 2)

Source Audio

The original audio with a distinct rhythmic structure.

SA Replacement (Layers 8-14)

Preserves the source's temporal structure (rhythm) while changing the instrument to violin.

SA Replacement (All Layers)

Strongly preserves structure, but some of the original drum timbre leaks through.

CA Replacement (Layers 8-14)

Ignores the source structure, generating a new violin melody based on the text prompt.

CA Replacement (All Layers)

Completely disregards the source structure, confirming CA's role in encoding text concepts.

Direct Generation

The target reference, generated from the prompt "a solo violin" with no source influence.

DDIM Inversion (No Replacement)

A baseline without attention control; fails to preserve the original rhythmic structure.

Ablation 2: Blues to Classical(from Figure 2)

Source Audio

The original audio in a 'Blues' style.

SA Replacement (Layers 8-14)

Effectively preserves the melodic contour of the blues track while shifting the style to classical.

SA Replacement (All Layers)

Over-preserves the source, with elements of the original blues style remaining.

CA Replacement (Layers 8-14)

Largely ignores the source melody, generating a new piece in the 'Classical' style.

CA Replacement (All Layers)

The output has no structural resemblance to the source, behaving like direct generation.

Direct Generation

The target reference, generated from the prompt "a typical classical music".

DDIM Inversion (No Replacement)

The baseline fails to maintain the melodic structure during the style transfer.

Ablation 3: Clarinet to Trombone

Source Audio

The original clarinet recording.

SA Replacement (Layers 8-14)

The optimal result: structure is preserved, and timbre is successfully changed to trombone.

SA Replacement (All Layers)

Structure is preserved, but the sound is a mix of clarinet and trombone.

CA Replacement (Layers 8-14)

The edit fails to keep the source melody, creating a new trombone piece.

CA Replacement (All Layers)

The result is entirely untethered from the source audio's structure.

Direct Generation

Target reference for "a solo trombone".

DDIM Inversion (No Replacement)
